Project Overview

About OptiCrop Engine

OptiCrop is developed to resolve uncertainties in farming decisions. By leveraging supervised machine learning (Logistic Regression), the engine matches complex soil and atmospheric variables to appropriate crop classes with high statistical confidence.

Soil Nutrients

Monitors Nitrogen (N), Phosphorous (P), and Potassium (K) content of the soil in mg/kg. These three macronutrients are critical for crop growth and development.

Climate Factors

Analyzes environmental conditions such as Temperature (in °C), relative Humidity (in %), and Rainfall (in mm) to identify seasonal compatibility.

Soil Acidity

Reads the soil pH value, which influences nutrient availability and biological activity, ensuring crops are placed in chemically friendly soil.

Model Performance & Architecture

The model is trained on a public dataset comprising 2,200 observation records mapping agricultural attributes. After filtering statistical outliers via Interquartile Range (IQR), a Logistic Regression model is fitted to perform 22-class classification. The model attains an overall 98% classification accuracy.
